# Trader Behavior vs Market Sentiment — Summary

## Objective
Study how trader performance and behavior change based on market sentiment (Fear vs Greed).

## Approach
- Cleaned and merged trader and sentiment datasets.
- Created behavioral metrics such as win rate, leverage, and trade frequency.
- Compared performance across sentiment conditions.
- Segmented traders based on behavior.
- Built a predictive model to estimate profitability.

## Findings
- Traders show different performance under Fear vs Greed conditions.
- Trade frequency increases during strong sentiment periods.
- High leverage traders exhibit greater volatility.
- Predictive model achieved 61% accuracy.

## Strategy Recommendations
- Reduce leverage during Fear days.
- Increase trading only for consistent performers.
- Monitor trade size during volatile sentiment.

## Conclusion
Market sentiment significantly influences trader behavior and performance. Risk-adjusted strategies can improve trading outcomes.
